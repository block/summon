## Composition

A routine makes a repeatable task short and easy to begin again. Reach for this
surface when the user's first question is "what are the few things I do each time,
and how do I just start?" — a watering rhythm, a weekly tidy, a recurring prep — not
when the task is a one-off next move (that is the [planner](../planner)), a
multi-step plan to move through once (that is the [staged-plan](../staged-plan)), or
a choice between options (that is the [comparison](../comparison)).

**Tiny routine checklist.** Keep repeatable routines short, tactile, and easy to
begin: a small checklist inside a soft [note panel](../note-panels) on
`--garden-note-paper` rounded to `--garden-panel-radius`, with the one thing to do
first marked by a single `--garden-chosen-dot` (sized at `--garden-chosen-marker`,
haloed by `--garden-chosen-ring`) — no habit dashboards, analytics, streak
counters, or gamified productivity. The list stays
short enough to start today, and the surface stays calm — a routine should feel
like glancing at a familiar note, not opening a tracking app.

**Gentle reminder rows.** Carry recurring reminders as gentle rows rather than
urgent alerts — soft, scannable, and easy to skip when not needed today. Use the
airy spacing ladder and [gentle depth rules](../gentle-depth) so the rows breathe
and never stack into a dense dashboard of equal cards.

Readiness, timing, season, and energy ride on the [badge system](../badges) —
`--garden-chip-radius` pills faced with `--garden-chip-face`, inked in
`--garden-chip-ink`, edged with `--garden-chip-edge` — labelled Ready, Needs one
thing, Season, Timing, used only when the state helps. Each routine still
resolves into concrete verbs the user can do today, and lowers pressure by making it
fine to do only the parts that matter this time.
