# Garden Notes Curation

## Promoted Signals

- Garden Notes should make personal planning feel startable, not optimized.
- The first useful move should be visible before supporting notes.
- Warmth is a pacing tool: rounded notes, soft green emphasis, gentle shadows, and forgiving labels should lower pressure.
- Concrete context matters. People, materials, dates, energy, uncertainty, and constraints from the prompt should remain visible.
- Personal comparisons should prefer good-enough recommendations over severe winners.
- Badges are useful when they carry state: time, effort, confidence, readiness, season, energy, or waiting.
- What can wait is part of the design, not leftover content.

## Curation Cautions

- Do not turn "garden" into literal botanical decoration unless the task itself calls for it.
- Do not add motivational slogans where a concrete next step would help more.
- Do not overuse now/next/later when the task is already tiny.
- Do not let soft notes become equal-weight card soup.
- Do not import Editorial Mono's severe verdict language into low-stakes personal planning.

## Exemplar Curation Hold

- No image exemplars are approved for Garden Notes yet.
- Do not promote agent-created or hand-built screenshots into `inventory.exemplars` until a human curator accepts the surface.
- Strong candidates should still prove specific context, one next small step, good-enough comparison, pressure release, and useful state badges.
