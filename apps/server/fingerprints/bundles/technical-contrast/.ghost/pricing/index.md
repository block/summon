## Composition

A pricing surface helps choose a tier, plan, or capacity by exposing shared
criteria. Reach for this surface when the user's first question is "what does each
tier cost and how do capabilities compare?" — pricing pages, capacity calculators,
model/plan matrices — not when the job is to introduce the platform (that is the
[landing](../landing)), to prove credibility at gravity (that is
[proof](../proof)), or to complete a configuration workflow (that is
[workflow](../workflow)).

**Left-sidebar pricing navigation.** Dense pricing and capacity pages pair a quiet
left category rail with a main white data sheet; active category bold or darker,
inactive muted, hairline dividers between groups. On mobile, collapse groups into
large plain accordions before the table.

**Pricing table sheet.** Pricing, model, capacity, and capability comparisons read
as crisp white sheets with uppercase-mono muted headers on a pale rail, geometric-
sans body cells with aligned numeric columns, and 1px hairline separators — no
shadows, winner badges, or decorative artwork inside the sheet. Keep shared
criteria visible and parallel across every option using the same criteria names;
never make the user infer comparable facts from differently structured cards. Omit
signal artwork entirely here — the data carries the page.

The sheets and tinted stat tiles follow the
[pricing-table and data-sheet system](../data-sheets); the category rail, column
headers, and segmented tabs follow the [mono label system](../mono-labels). Where a
table grows dense, let it scroll, stack with preserved row labels, or collapse into
category accordions rather than swap to unrelated cards.
