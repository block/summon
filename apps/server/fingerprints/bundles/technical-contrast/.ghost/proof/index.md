## Composition

A proof surface argues credibility at gravity. Reach for this surface when the
user's first question is "why should I believe this is fast, safe, or proven?" —
research write-ups, benchmark pages, security/compliance proof, model-serving
evidence — not when the job is to introduce the platform (the [landing](../landing)),
to compare cost and capacity (the [pricing](../pricing)), or to complete a
workflow (the [workflow](../workflow)).

**Midnight proof plane + dark proof-card grid.** This surface leans on the
near-black midnight plane — a strong display claim, mono eyebrow, muted recognition
rail, and dark-on-dark proof cards bounded by dark-soft hairlines. Each card starts
with a mono tag, then a sentence-case title and concise body, and pairs the proof
point with an operational implication — never bright cards floating on the dark
plane or neon glows.

**Technical update card.** Benchmark, certification, model-serving, and research
updates appear as compact flat proof cards — short uppercase mono category badge
(BENCHMARK, SECURITY, RESEARCH, CAPABILITY), bold sentence-case headline, one short
implication sentence, optional neutral graphic plane, hairline bounds, small radius,
and a quiet next-step action. Connect the update to a capability, risk, cost, or
decision. Metrics and proof must explain why the primary claim is credible — pair
large numbers with labels and implications, and avoid orphaned stats or testimonial
fragments.

The midnight plane follows the [contrast-plane system](../contrast-planes); the
cards follow the [proof-card system](../proof-cards); category badges and claims
follow the [mono labels](../mono-labels). A large signal artwork may anchor energy
here when it clarifies proof, but stays optional and subordinate to the evidence.
