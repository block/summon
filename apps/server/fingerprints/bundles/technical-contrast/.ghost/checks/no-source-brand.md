description: Generated surfaces must not reuse any real technical-platform's brand identity — they are source-informed, never source-branded.

---

Technical Contrast is informed by public technical-platform research but must stay
neutral and fictionalized. Reject the generated surface if it reuses any **real
source's** identity:

- a real platform, company, or product name, domain, slogan, wordmark, or logo;
- real customer logos, conference marks, or recognition badges used as content;
- real model names, exact pricing values, or verbatim source headlines and copy;
- proprietary screenshots or proprietary hero/signal artwork copied as an asset.

The fingerprint informs **composition only** — contrast planes, white data sheets,
midnight proof planes, mono eyebrows, pricing sheets, proof cards, signal-artwork
*placement*. Identity content must be fictional, user-supplied, or derived from the
user's prompt: name the generated product, topic, or task from the prompt, not the
source inspiration. Use generic placeholders for customer logos, conference marks,
model names, and pricing values unless the prompt supplies them, and translate
terminal-brand gestures into a generic low-contrast sign-off or omit them. A
masthead, recognition rail, or terminal sign-off is a composition role — scale and
placement only, never a copied mark, domain, or slogan.
