## Promoted durable signals

- **Pale technical landing plane.** A technical cloud/platform can open on an airy pale canvas rather than defaulting to a dark hero.
- **Claim before artifact.** The headline, short technical deck, and action pair appear before product screenshots, tables, or research cards.
- **Compact mono action system.** CTAs, badges, tabs, and technical labels use short uppercase mono text in small-radius rectangles.
- **Large optional signal artwork.** Abstract warm-to-cool signal art can support the landing claim, but should remain one large object or cropped plane, not a repeated UI motif.
- **Muted proof rail.** Recognition, customer, or credibility marks are quiet support below the claim rather than primary hierarchy.
- **White technical data sheet.** Pricing, model, capacity, and capability comparisons use table or matrix structure with visible shared criteria.
- **Left rail plus table for dense comparison.** Desktop pricing evidence supports a quiet left category rail paired with a white data sheet.
- **Segmented technical tabs.** Model families and pricing modes use compact segmented controls with one clear active state.
- **Aligned numeric columns.** Costs, limits, latency, throughput, and capacity values earn trust through alignment and row consistency.
- **Midnight research plane.** Research, benchmark, and credibility content can switch to a dark plane with one strong claim and muted institutional proof.
- **Product UI proof mockup.** Product pages use large interface mockups as proof of capability or workflow, not scattered decorative screenshots.
- **Mobile crop, not miniaturization.** On narrow screens, signal artwork may crop dramatically and dense category systems may collapse into accordions; CTAs stay rectangular and claim-first order remains intact.
- **Technical update cards.** Benchmark, certification, and research updates can appear as flat cards with mono category badges, bold technical headlines, and concise implication copy.

## Rejected source-specific material

Do not promote or copy:

- Together AI name, logo, wordmark, domain, source navigation labels, source slogans, or exact product copy.
- Customer logos, conference logos, institution marks, author names, article titles, model names, exact benchmark values, exact pricing values, or source screenshots.
- The exact 3D signal artwork geometry, exact product UI screenshots, dashboard chrome, avatar/account details, or source brand color relationships.
- Logo-in-card treatment from the blog page; translate into neutral typographic proof artwork if needed.
- Source taxonomy as universal law. Categories such as inference, compute, model shaping, research, and developers are evidence for technical grouping, not required labels.

## Page observations

### Home page

The home surface opens with a black announcement strip, white nav capsules, a pale blue-white canvas, and a large sentence-case claim with a muted secondary phrase. Two compact rectangular CTAs sit below the deck. A large abstract signal object occupies the right side with tiny technical callout labels. A quiet trusted-by rail closes the hero.

Use this as evidence for `technical-plane-landing`, `mono-eyebrow-system`, `rectangular-cta-system`, `muted-proof-rail`, and mobile signal-crop behavior.

### Pricing page

The pricing surface pairs a quiet left sidebar grouped by technical domain with a main content area containing a mono `PRICING` badge, page title, segmented model-category tabs, a control/toggle row, and a white table sheet. The table has a pale header, sortable-looking mono column labels, dense rows, aligned input/output price columns, cached-price subcopy, and hairline separators. On mobile, dense category groups collapse into large accordion rows instead of squeezing the full table into the first viewport.

Use this as evidence for `left-sidebar-pricing-navigation`, `pricing-table-sheet`, `segmented-technical-tabs`, `comparison-criteria-stay-visible`, and `mobile-technical-collapse`.

### Research page

The research surface uses a midnight plane and dark nav shell. A compact mono badge sits above a centered display claim, with one emphasized periwinkle phrase. A muted recognition rail supports the claim. Dark research areas and recognized-paper cards continue the proof context below.

Use this as evidence for `midnight-proof-plane`, `dark-proof-card-grid`, and `proof-supports-claim`.

### Product capability pages

Serverless inference, GPU clusters, and fine-tuning pages share a white hero with a compact mono product badge, centered technical claim, concise explanation, rectangular CTA pair, and a large product UI proof mockup inside a pastel signal panel. The mockups show model pages, deployment controls, configuration choices, and summary panels.

Use this as evidence for `technical-form-sheet`, `code-editor-breakout`, `technical-plane-landing`, and `proof-supports-claim`.

### Blog page

The blog surface uses a pale canvas, large simple heading, horizontal card row, small mono category badges, bold technical article titles, short excerpts, and small square carousel controls. Card art often contains source branding; neutral exemplars must remove logos and replace exact titles with generic benchmark/update content.

Use this as evidence for `technical-update-card` only after abstracting away logos, exact headlines, and source art.

## Exemplar translation rules

- Source screenshot becomes a neutral derived exemplar or curation note, never a committed raw source asset by default.
- Source wordmark/logos become generic product placeholders or are omitted.
- Customer and recognition logos become muted generic proof placeholders.
- Exact model names and prices become plausible placeholder rows tied to the generated prompt.
- Source product screenshots become simplified neutral interface mockups.
- Source headline and blog copy becomes task-specific neutral copy.
- Signal artwork becomes abstract geometry with no copied shape, no source mark, and no repeated decorative motifs.

## Review stance

Together AI is useful because it demonstrates an unusually complete technical-platform composition system: pale technical hero, dense pricing sheet, midnight research plane, product proof mockups, and mobile collapse behavior. It should strengthen Technical Contrast's evidence base, but it must not make the bundle look like Together AI. The fingerprint remains source-agnostic.
