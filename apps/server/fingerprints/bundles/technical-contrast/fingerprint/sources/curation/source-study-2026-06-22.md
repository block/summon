# Technical Contrast source study

This curation note distills online research into a public AI-infrastructure web
surface plus a user-provided design analysis into an agnostic Ghost fingerprint
for generated Summon surfaces. It intentionally avoids source product names,
domains, logos, literal wordmarks, proprietary copy, screenshots, and copied hero
artwork.

## Research targets

- Home landing page: pale technical hero, black announcement strip, white nav
  capsule, left-aligned sentence-case claim, compact uppercase mono CTAs, large
  abstract signal artwork, muted trusted-by logo rail.
- Pricing page: white technical sheet, left category rail, mono page badge,
  segmented tabs, hairline data table, pale header row, dense model rows, aligned
  numeric price columns, quiet help card.
- Research page: midnight plane, dark nav shell, mono research badge, large
  white/periwinkle display claim, muted recognition rail, dark proof/research
  cards below.
- Product/model pages: technical capability claims, implementation detail,
  model/category navigation, and code/configuration breakouts as proof rather
  than decoration.
- Contact/workflow pages: white form surfaces, small-radius inputs, mono labels,
  black primary actions, and restrained explanatory copy.

## Promoted durable signals

- Contrast planes: pale/white technical surfaces for product and data, midnight
  planes for proof and research.
- White technical data sheets: tables and matrices for pricing, model, capacity,
  latency, limits, and capability comparisons.
- Uppercase mono label system: eyebrows, CTAs, tabs, table headers, compact
  metrics, technical badges, and metadata.
- Tight geometric sans narrative: sentence-case headlines, compact body copy,
  slightly negative display tracking, no mono paragraphs.
- Hairline depth: 1px dividers, pale table headers, dark-on-dark card borders,
  minimal or absent shadows.
- Small-radius rectangles: buttons, tabs, cards, inputs, table furniture, and
  badges stay lightly rounded rather than fully pill-shaped.
- Optional signal artwork: when present, it is large, abstract, warm-to-cool, and
  secondary to the claim. It is not a general UI color system.
- Muted proof rails: logo/recognition/metric rows are quiet and subordinate to
  the main claim.
- Dense but humane comparison: compact row rhythm with aligned numeric columns
  and visible shared criteria.

## Rejected source-specific material

- Source product names, domains, slogans, wordmarks, and navigation labels when
  the user's prompt does not supply them.
- Literal hero artwork, screenshots, customer logo bars, model names, pricing
  values, and proprietary source copy.
- The exact source brand mark or terminal footer wordmark. Translate the idea
  into a generic low-contrast sign-off only when useful.
- Any instruction that makes generated output appear to be the source company or
  a source-owned product.

## Curation cautions

- Do not make the language decorative-effect-led. The durable system is contrast,
  type, hairlines, and data structure.
- Do not over-darken every hero. The researched home page supports pale technical
  landing planes; the research page supports midnight proof planes.
- Do not turn optional signal artwork into repeated icons, badges, CTA fills,
  underlines, or small category swatches.
- Do not replace structured tables with generic SaaS pricing cards when shared
  criteria are the task.
- Do not produce neon AI SaaS: no glows, glassmorphism, rainbow dashboards, or
  large soft shadows.
- Do not set long body or explanation copy in monospace; mono is for orientation
  and controls.
- Do not treat muted logos, badges, or proof rails as the primary hierarchy.

## Page observations

### Home landing page

The strongest observed landing pattern is a pale technical plane with a black
announcement strip and a white navigation capsule. The headline is large,
sentence-case, left-aligned, and partly muted for emphasis. CTAs are compact,
rectangular, and uppercase mono. A large abstract signal object sits on the right
and communicates technical energy without becoming UI chrome. A muted trusted-by
rail closes the hero.

### Pricing page

The strongest observed data pattern is a white sheet with a left category rail,
mono page badge, segmented tabs, and a hairline table. The table header is pale
and muted; body rows are dense with model names and aligned prices. This is the
best evidence that pricing, model, and capacity comparisons should stay tabular.

### Research page

The strongest observed proof pattern is a midnight plane with a centered display
claim and one emphasized periwinkle phrase. A muted recognition rail sits below
the claim. Research/proof cards continue the midnight material with dark-on-dark
surfaces and subdued borders.

### Product and implementation pages

Product pages reinforce technical proof through capability groupings, model or
compute categories, and code/configuration panels. Code mockups should be
minimal, dark, text-led, and tied to a nearby claim.

### Contact and workflow pages

Workflow pages support white sheet composition, small-radius fields, mono labels,
and one clear black primary action. Explanatory copy should clarify constraints
or fit, not become onboarding fluff.

## Exemplar translation rules

- Source screenshot becomes a neutral derived exemplar.
- Source wordmark becomes a generic placeholder label or omitted mark.
- Source product copy becomes task-specific neutral copy.
- Source signal art becomes abstract signal artwork with no copied geometry.
- Source model names and prices become plausible placeholder technical rows.
- Source logo rails become muted generic recognition or proof rails.
- Source brand colors become semantic Technical Contrast tokens, not a literal
  brand asset package.
