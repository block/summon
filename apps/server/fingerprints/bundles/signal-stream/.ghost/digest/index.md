## Composition

A digest curates editorial priority. Reach for this surface when the user's first
question is "what are the most important stories, ranked by editorial priority?"
— a feature digest, an editorial front page, a review roundup, a section index —
not when the job is to show live ordered updates as they arrive (that is the
[stream](../stream)) or to report a single state or recommendation (that is the
[briefing](../briefing)).

**Lead-image headline slab / compact supporting grid.** A feature lead owns the
first read — an image-first block with an attached or overlapping [headline
slab](../type-system) — then compact two-column supporting rows with thumbnails,
hairline separators, and demoted metadata. The [display shout](../type-system)
anchors the lead; supporting stories whisper beneath it.

**Dense editorial grid.** Lay out ~1280px max width, 24px mobile / 48px desktop
outer padding, 3–4 columns with feature modules spanning columns by priority, and
32–64px dark vertical breaks between sections as palette cleansers. Feature
modules earn their span by priority; not every story is equal weight.

**Saturated section leads.** A high-priority story becomes a solid saturated
[hazard tile](../canvas) on the black canvas, printed-on-the-field, marking the
editorial lead — no more than a few saturated accents per viewport.

The tiles, slabs, and supporting grid follow the [tile system](../tiles); the
shout, kicker, and demoted metadata follow the [type system](../type-system);
section selectors and any CTA follow the [controls](../controls). On mobile,
collapse the grid to a single column while preserving lead hierarchy, metadata,
and saturation.
