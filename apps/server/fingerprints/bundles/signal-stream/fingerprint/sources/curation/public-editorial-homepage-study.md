# Source Study: Public Technology Editorial Homepage

Date: 2026-06-22
Source inspected: a public technology editorial homepage
Bundle: Signal Stream

## Curation stance

This source study is evidence for composition, hierarchy, responsive behavior, and feed pacing only. It is not evidence for copying publisher identity. Raw source screenshots, logos, article titles, author names, ads, and section names are not portable exemplar assets for this bundle.

Signal Stream promotes source-informed neutral redraws as evidence. Those redraws should use fictional content, generic marks, and agnostic labels.

## Durable signals promoted

- One dominant editorial lead should be visually unmistakable.
- Mode controls can be large rounded segmented pills with one saturated active segment and muted inactive segments.
- A latest/feed column can stay dense when each item has compact metadata, short body copy, links, and hairline separation.
- Supporting stories should be compact and visibly subordinate to the lead.
- Mobile collapse should preserve the mode control, lead hierarchy, metadata, and saturation instead of shrinking everything into unreadable cards.
- Depth is graphic and flat: rules, separators, strong contrast, and color blocks carry hierarchy.
- Metadata is useful when it shows author/source role, time, count, section, status, or update order.

## Signals not promoted

- Publisher wordmark, masthead, logo construction, or brand name.
- Exact article titles, author names, story topics, or source copy.
- Advertisement creative, sponsored modules, or ad placement conventions.
- Proprietary font names or source-specific asset references.
- Exact homepage layout as a template. Only abstract roles should survive: lead stack, latest rail, mobile story stack, section digest, saturated interruption tile.

## Evidence strength

- `weighted`: neutral redraws derived from the lead stack, latest rail, and mobile top story may strongly steer generation when paired with prose and composition refs.
- `supporting`: section digest and saturated interruption examples help broaden generation but should not override the core stream/lead patterns.
- `excluded`: raw source screenshots and ads should not be canonical generation input.

## Rights and portability boundary

Do not commit raw screenshots from the public source unless rights are explicitly approved. Prefer redrawn, fictionalized exemplars stored under `examples/core/`. Core fingerprint files should cite bundle-local neutral exemplars and curation notes, not local machine paths or raw browser captures.
