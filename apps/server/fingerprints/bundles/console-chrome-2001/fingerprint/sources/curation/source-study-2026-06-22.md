# Console Chrome 2001 Curation

## Source Basis

This draft was synthesized from the user-supplied design-analysis file at `/Users/nahiyan/Downloads/DESIGN-nintendo-2001.md` and public research on Nintendo.com in 2001. The public Web Design Museum page `https://www.webdesignmuseum.org/gallery/nintendo-2001` identifies the historical source and exposes an 830×700 screenshot (`https://www.webdesignmuseum.org/uploaded/timeline/nintendo/nintendo-2001.png`) that corroborates the attached study: mascot masthead, carbon command navigation, pale secondary strip, a Game Boy Advance hero plate, stacked news rows, featured thumbnail grid, right-side action buttons, poll panel, left vertical tabs, and a compact footer slab.

The portable fingerprint intentionally avoids product-specific Nintendo names, characters, wordmarks, screenshots, console images, third-party characters, ratings marks, exact labels, and literal source structure. It promotes the reusable design language: Y2K console hardware rendered as a webpage.

## Promoted Signals

- The generated surface should feel like a physical game console faceplate, not a modern website with retro colors.
- The shell is cool periwinkle molded plastic: canvas plates, pale-sky secondary strips, platinum inset rows, white fields, and chrome-indigo bevel/shadow edges.
- The command layer is carbon navy with halftone or dot-matrix texture: nav, side buttons, tabs, and footer should read as hardware controls.
- Warm hues are semantically rationed:
  - nav gold for primary command words;
  - amber for tools, badges, info tabs, and utility chips;
  - signal orange for forward arrows, open/launch/continue, and submit actions.
- Dense fixed-canvas composition is part of the voice. It should feel packed with useful modules and small labels rather than airy.
- Uppercase Arial Bold labels are the silkscreened controller legends of the system. Body copy stays small and plain.
- Hero plates carry box-art treatment: textured or pictorial field, heavy outlined display title, hard drop shadow, and orange advance cue.
- Bevels are hard and graphic. Use inset highlights, bottom/right indigo seams, dotted dividers, and clipped/chamfered edges instead of blurred shadows.
- A small original mascot or speech bubble can add character when useful, but it must not recreate protected characters or source poses.

## Curation Cautions

- Do not include the Nintendo logo, Mario, Pikachu, Pokémon, Game Boy, ESRB marks, exact copyright text, historical screenshots, or Nintendo-owned copy unless the user explicitly provides those as content for a specific transformation.
- Do not turn the fingerprint into generic retro pixel art. Pixel/dot texture is secondary to the molded chrome, command bars, and dense module grid.
- Do not use orange/amber as general decoration. Warm color must point to a tool, action, nav, badge, or forward path.
- Do not soften the whole interface into rounded pastel cards. Sharp/chamfered geometry with limited round controls is the signature.
- Do not add modern soft shadows, glassmorphism, neon cyberpunk glows, or luxury whitespace.
- Do not overfit to one 2001 homepage screenshot. Adapt the language to landing pages, directories, briefs, and workflows.
- No canonical image exemplars are approved. Public screenshots remain research evidence, not reusable generation assets.
