## Composition

A directory helps browse and find within a collection. Reach for this surface
when the user's first question is "which one am I looking for, and how do I find
it?" — a games or product finder, a category listing, an archive index — not when
the job is to announce one title (that is the [launch](../launch)), to monitor a
running system of modules (that is the [dashboard](../dashboard)), or to complete
a form or playful tool (that is the [utility](../utility)).

**Thumbnail tile grid.** Use tiny framed thumbnail tiles in tight two-up or
three-up grids, clipped into dark or periwinkle frames carrying the
`--chrome-bevel-hard` two-tone seam, with captions and orange arrows lit by
`--chrome-led-glow` only for actionable tile moves. Use generated abstract art,
icons, or task-supplied imagery — never borrowed historical assets.

**Tool-chip search cluster.** Group search and filter controls like hardware
utilities — a `--chrome-inset-input` white well pressed into the faceplate, a
compact native-style select, an amber Go chip beaded with `--chrome-led-pip`, and
small tips/link text inside or near the command layer, with controls labeled
directly rather than by placeholder alone. This finder is the surface's primary
way in.

**Side-mounted vertical tabs.** Optional left-edge vertical carbon tabs textured
by `--chrome-halftone-carbon` clip into the chassis as rotated or stacked labels
for rankings, archives, categories, or ratings, parted by
`--chrome-divider-dotted-v` — secondary navigation or status, never primary
reading content.

The search cluster and tile actions follow the [control system](../controls); the
category tabs and finder strip follow the [command and navigation
system](../command-nav); each tile and listing carries a section-label or rating
stamp from the [badge and section-label system](../badges).
