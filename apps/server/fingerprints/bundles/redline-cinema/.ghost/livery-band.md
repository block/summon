## Composition

Red is voltage, not palette — and the livery band is the one place red is allowed
to fill a whole region. At most one full-width red accent band appears per page,
as a dramatic interruption for a major statement, event status, or transition.

**One band, restrained type.** The band carries white, large, restrained text and
no extra colors — a single editorial statement, a launch moment, an event status,
or a transition between acts. It is omitted on compact task surfaces where its
drama would feel like noise; a [directory](directory) sheet or a dense
[catalog](catalog) listing rarely earns one.

This band contrasts deliberately with the scarce red of the [control
system](controls) — where controls use red as a single high-voltage CTA fill, the
livery band uses red as the surface itself — and it is the one intentional break
in the [hairline and brightness depth](depth) calm. Use it once or not at all.
