## Composition

Cinema is the chrome. Full-bleed cinematic imagery or image placeholders carry
the drama while navigation, headings, and CTAs stay quiet.

**Full-bleed cinematic hero.** A viewport-scale dark image field anchors the
surface, running edge-to-edge at `--redline-frame-bleed` (zero inset) over the
`--redline-canvas-warm` stage, with a restrained display headline near the lower
edge or in a tight dark band below, a compact deck, and at most two sharp actions;
keep navigation minimal so the image is the event. On mobile, crop vertically
while preserving the cinematic frame. Drop the headline zone under
`--redline-frame-overlay` for legibility — a near-black legibility scrim, never
decorative glass — and lean on photographic atmosphere rather than shadow.

**Image-first feature card.** Edge-to-edge media occupies the top or majority of
the card, held square at `--redline-frame-radius` (the image plate is never
softened), no shadow, with tight title and body copy beneath, laid out in two-up
or three-up grids rather than crowded masonry. The image leads; the copy
supports.

**Derived image-placeholder discipline.** When prompt imagery is absent, use dark
gradients, cropped light streaks, abstract silhouettes, material detail, or
generic performance photography descriptions — never invented source-branded
vehicles, marks, or model photography. Prefer no image over a fake brand image.
Reserve the large cinematic image slot with a gradient or dark overlay rather than
inventing logos or proprietary artwork.

This image system pairs with the [scarce-red controls](controls) that sit quietly
over or beneath the frame, and rests on the [hairline and brightness
depth](depth) system rather than shadow elevation. Where the focal subject is a
number rather than a photograph, that is the [spec system](spec-system)'s job.
