Redline Cinema is informed by public luxury-performance editorial research but must
stay neutral and source-agnostic. Reject the generated surface if it reintroduces
any **real automotive brand's** identity:

- a real manufacturer, marque, or sub-brand name, wordmark, or logo;
- an animal mark, shield, crest, or other proprietary emblem shape;
- a real model name, trim name, chassis code, or racing-team identifier;
- a brand slogan, tagline, or licensed source font name;
- proprietary or recognizable source vehicle photography presented as content.

The fingerprint informs **composition only** — cinematic heroes, scarce red
voltage, sharp rectangular geometry, large-number spec grids, event rows, white
catalog sheets, and the single red livery band. Identity content must be fictional,
user-supplied, or task-derived: name the product, event, or category from the
user's prompt, and otherwise use generic roles such as performance marque, private
collection, track session, studio archive, or service directory. When imagery is
absent, use neutral cinematic placeholders, dark gradients, or abstract engineered
crops — never an invented source-like badge, livery, silhouette, or model photo.
Prefer no image over a fake brand image when provenance is unclear.
