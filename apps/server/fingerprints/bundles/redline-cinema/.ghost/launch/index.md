## Composition

A launch reveals and stirs. Reach for this surface when the user's first question
is "what is this, and why should I feel it?" — a product reveal, a model launch,
an event teaser, a cinematic landing — not when the job is to prove performance
data (that is the [spec](../spec)), to list and transact across many items (the
[catalog](../catalog)), to find a location or service (the
[directory](../directory)), or to browse a family of products (the
[lineup](../lineup)).

**Cinema first.** Open with a full-bleed [cinematic hero](../cinematic-image): a
viewport-scale dark image field running edge-to-edge at `--redline-frame-bleed`
over the `--redline-canvas-warm` stage, dropped under `--redline-frame-overlay`,
with a restrained display headline near the lower edge or in a tight dark band
below, a compact deck, and at most two sharp actions from the [control
system](../controls). Keep navigation minimal so the image is the event; on
mobile, crop vertically while preserving the cinematic frame.

**One claim, then tighten.** The first region establishes cinematic atmosphere
and a concise editorial claim — eyebrow, headline, one deck, at most two actions —
before any dense detail. After the hero, tighten into editorial body sections so
the page feels edited, not empty, using [dark-to-body pacing](../depth) with large
96–128px breaks between acts.

**One dramatic interruption.** A launch is the surface most likely to earn the
[single red livery band](../livery-band) — one `--redline-livery-band-h` sweep of
`--redline-livery-band` cut full-width for the reveal moment or a transition, or
the thinner `--redline-voltage-stripe` in its place. Use it once or not at all;
everywhere else `--redline-voltage` stays scarce.
