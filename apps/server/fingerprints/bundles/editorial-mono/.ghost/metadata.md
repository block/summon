## Composition

The mono family is the metadata voice of the surface. Folios, issue labels,
timestamps, ranks, criteria labels, and compact captions read as a disciplined
uppercase mono system with measured `--tracking-label`, kept useful rather than
decorative. Controls, inputs, labels, and metadata all route through the mono
family while the serif workhorse carries paragraphs and headings; `strong`
emphasis goes heavy rather than colored.

**Mono metadata system.** Use mono for the small, exact furniture of an editorial
page — the folio in a corner, an issue or section label, a timestamp, a rank, a
criteria label, a compact caption. Keep it uppercase with measured tracking and
short, parallel wording so it scans as a labeling system, not a paragraph voice.
The copy atoms are exact and reusable across the surface: Verdict, Evidence, Risk,
Caveat, Recommendation, Accepted tradeoff, What changed, What to watch, Rank,
Criteria. State variants the system must express stay legible: selected option,
recommended path, waiting, blocked risk, ready evidence, empty data, error, and
incomplete comparison. Reserve pill radius for compact mono labels and controls
only, never broad panels.

These labels frame the [masthead / deck / folio shell](masthead), tag the
[ruled evidence bands](evidence) with their short uppercase headers, and mark the
[editorial close](close) with a compact recommendation or rank label.
