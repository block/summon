## Composition

Editorial Mono allows at most one inverse black panel per surface, reserved for the
recommendation, verdict, or strongest contrast moment. The rest of the surface stays
on paper neutrals with ruled structure and crisp square corners, so the single
inverse panel reads as the page's one decisive emphasis rather than a styling motif.

**Single inverse ink panel.** Render the panel with `--color-accent` ink fill and
`--color-accent-fg` paper text, keep its corners square (`--radius-sm`), and place it
where the page most needs to commit — the verdict headline moment, the recommended
option, or the strongest claim. Never spend the inverse panel on decoration, a second
panel, or a competing region; if two moments compete for it, the verdict wins and the
other demotes to a [ruled evidence band](evidence). Emphasis is made from this inverse
ink, rules, type, spacing, and alignment before any color.

The inverse panel is the loudest the surface gets; it pairs with the
[editorial close](close) when the close itself carries the recommendation, and it
contrasts with the paper-neutral [evidence bands](evidence) that justify it.
