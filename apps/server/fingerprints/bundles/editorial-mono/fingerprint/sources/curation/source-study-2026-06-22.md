# Editorial Mono Curation

## Promoted Signals

- Editorial Mono should behave like a decision surface, not a decorative newspaper theme.
- The first read should expose the verdict, recommendation, or central claim.
- Monochrome is the material system: ink, paper, rules, type, alignment, and spacing carry expression.
- Comparisons need shared criteria that line up visibly.
- Evidence should be dense enough to feel edited, but structured enough to scan.
- Inverse black panels are high-signal moments and should be rare.
- Metadata is useful when it frames evidence: folios, timestamps, ranks, labels, and compact captions.

## Curation Cautions

- Do not turn cream paper into cozy beige lifestyle warmth.
- Do not treat "editorial" as license for oversized type that clips, overlaps, or ignores host chrome.
- Do not add color to solve hierarchy when ink, rules, type, and alignment can solve it.
- Do not make austere surfaces empty; the system should still help the user decide.
- Taste rules here are primarily advisory generation steering. Deterministic checks should stay narrow until drift patterns are observed.

## Exemplar Curation Hold

- No image exemplars are approved for Editorial Mono yet.
- Do not promote agent-created or hand-built screenshots into `inventory.exemplars` until a human curator accepts the surface.
- Strong candidates should still prove verdict-first hierarchy, aligned evidence, monochrome material discipline, and a useful close.
