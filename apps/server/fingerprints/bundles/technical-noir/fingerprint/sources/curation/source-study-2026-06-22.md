## Durable Signals Promoted

- The surface should feel like a developer reading/work environment rather than a conventional marketing dashboard.
- Technical claims should be followed quickly by proof: terminal state, product UI, agent plan, command output, setup trace, or operational rows.
- Warm dark neutrals are the brand material; do not replace them with pure black, cool gray, neon gradients, or colorful accent systems.
- Off-white is both primary text and primary action material; it is not supplemented by a separate chromatic brand accent.
- Terminal or code-like panels are the primary decorative vocabulary and should carry real content, examples, or operational evidence.
- Typography stays quiet: large display type is light-weight, tightly tracked, and sentence-like rather than billboard-heavy.
- Geometry is tight: buttons and cards are almost rectangular, with circular or pill shapes reserved for icon controls or compact state chips.
- Elevation comes from surface contrast and 1px warm hairlines; shadows and atmospheric glows are not part of the core language.
- Dense rows, tiles, and mockup panels should be legible and well-spaced without becoming soft lifestyle cards.
- Product/workflow alternatives can use a compact selector or tab rail that gives most of the space to the evidence frame.
- Setup and download surfaces should be functional: platform label, compatibility detail, command or action, and architecture chips.
- Trust, ecosystem, and integration signals should stay monochrome and secondary to product evidence.

## Signals Rejected

- Source product names, source copy, source screenshots, source logos, partner names, customer names, testimonials, and exact navigation labels.
- Recreating the researched site's current light-page skin as the default Technical Noir expression.
- Decorative gradients, glassmorphism, neon terminal nostalgia, cyberpunk color palettes, glow, or atmospheric backdrops.
- Cinematic noir styling: fog, spotlights, film grain, detective-board metaphors, dramatic shadows, smoked-glass panels, or moody darkness detached from task evidence.
- Over-rounded CTAs, consumer-app softness, pastel productivity styling, or generic SaaS card grids.
- Fake terminal panels that do not explain state, commands, outputs, tasks, agents, or evidence.
